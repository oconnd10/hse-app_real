Note on MRSA

- Infection -


MRSA infection should be suspected if:

• The patient has been previously colonised with MRSA
• The patient has recently been hospitalised
• The patient has been transferred from another hospital or long term care facility
• The patient is on a ward with a current outbreak of MRSA

For patients with serious/life threatening infection who are at risk from MRSA infection, empiric treatment with a glycopeptide (e.g. vancomycin or teicoplanin) is indicated in addition to the other components of therapy recommended in this guideline.

Please consult Microbiology if further advice required.

- Prophylaxis -


Patients who require surgery and have a history of MRSA colonization or infection without documented eradication should receive teicoplanin prophylaxis alone or in combination with other antibiotics active against other relevant pathogens.

Teicoplanin should also be considered if there is an appreciable risk that the patients MRSA carriage may have recurred or they come from a facility with a high prevalence of MRSA.
