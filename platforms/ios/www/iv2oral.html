IV to Oral Switch

C.O.M.S Box 1

Considerations for IV to oral switch
C.O.M.S
C   Clinical improvement

O   Oral route not comprimised
      •  Vomiting
      •  NPO
      •  Unconscious
      •  Swallowing Problems
      •  Severe diarrhoea
      •  Malabsorptive disorder
(if NG/PEG, please consult antimicrobial pharmacist).

M   Markers showing a trend towards normal
      Apyrexial: Temp >35.5 and <38 for 24hrs
      Plus not more than one of the following:
•  HR >90beats/min
•  Respiratory Rate >20breaths/min
•  BP unstable
•  WCC <4 OR >12 (if abnormal, a trend towards normal range and without neutropenia is acceptable)

S   Specific indication/deep-seated infection
Specific indications requiring prolonged IV;
     • Staph. aureus or Pseudomonas aeruginosa bacteraemia.
     • Severe necrotising soft tissue infections.
     • Severe infections during chemotherapy related neutropenia
     • Infected implants/Prostheses.
     • Meningitis/Encephalitis.
     • Intracranial abscesses
     • Mediastinitis
     • Infective Endocarditis
     • Exacerbation of Cystic Fibrosis/Bronchiectasis
     • Inadequately drained abscesses or empyema
     • Infections of foreign bodies e.g Central Line Infections
Examples of deep eared infections that may require an initial minimum 2 weeks of IV therapy;
        •  Liver abscess
        •  Osteomyelitis
        •  Septic arthritis
